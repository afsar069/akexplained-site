Terms of Use

Free to read, free to study from, and yours to keep. A few plain limits, written without legal fog.

Using this site

Everything on akexplained.com is free to read and free to use for your own study. Copy it into your notebook, translate it, rearrange it, quote it in an assignment — none of that requires my permission and all of it is the point.

Reusing the material

  • For study — freely. No credit needed, no restriction.
  • For teaching — yes, with credit. If you use a page with your own students, please mention where it came from.
  • Republishing as your own — no. Copying pages onto another website, channel or app as your own content is not permitted. If you want to work together rather than copy, write to me.

Student contributions

Work published in Your Creativity remains the property of the student who wrote it. Publishing here transfers no right to me. You may publish the same piece anywhere else at any time, and you may ask for it to be removed at any time — it will be removed without discussion.

You must only send work that is your own. Copied or AI-generated work presented as original is removed and the contributor is not published again.

Accuracy, and its limits

Every page here is checked against standard reference works, and nothing — no date, no critic, no quotation — is invented. But syllabi change, examination patterns are revised, and mistakes happen even with care.

So treat this site as study material, not as an official document. For anything that affects your registration, eligibility, examination form or result, the university or the conducting agency is the authority, not me. Where a page mentions an official body, always confirm against that body's own notification.

If you find an error, tell me. Factual corrections take priority and are usually fixed within a few days.

What this site is not

There is no paid tier, no course for sale, no coaching enrolment and no paid group. I will never ask you for money. If any account claiming to be AK Explained requests a payment from you, it is not me — please report it to me so that I can warn others.
