How these notes are arranged

Not alphabetically. English literature only makes sense in sequence — the Metaphysicals are a reaction to something, the Romantics are a reaction to that reaction, and the Modernists break a form that had to exist first. So the notes are arranged by age, and within each age by genre and then by individual text.

If you are preparing for a specific examination, start from your exam page instead and follow the links from there — it will filter this same material down to your syllabus and skip what you do not need.

How to actually use a note

Reading a note once and feeling that you understood it is the most common way students waste an evening. Understanding and remembering are different processes, and only the second one is tested.

  • Read the primary text first if you possibly can. Even a rough reading of the poem or the play makes the note three times more useful, because you will be recognising things instead of memorising them.
  • Close the page and write the argument from memory. Four or five lines, in your own words. Whatever you cannot write down, you have not learned yet.
  • Keep one notebook of your own condensed points, not a copy of the note. In the last week before the examination you will revise your notebook, not this website.
  • Return after three days, then after two weeks. Two short revisits beat one long study session, and this is the single cheapest improvement most candidates can make.
