Previous Year Questions

A past paper is a document about the examiner's mind. Read it in your first month, not your last.

What a previous-year paper is really for

Most students treat past papers as a final rehearsal — something to attempt in the last fortnight to check whether the preparation worked. That is the least valuable use of them.

A past paper is a document about the examiner's mind. It shows you which authors return every year and which appeared once in a decade, how deep the questions actually go, how much of the paper is recognition and how much is reasoning, and which parts of a large syllabus are effectively decorative. All of that is information you want in your first month, not your last.

A method that works

  1. Attempt before you study. Take a paper you have never seen, on a syllabus you have only half-covered, and attempt it honestly. The score will be poor and that is not the point.
  2. Mark it yourself, question by question. Against each wrong answer write one word: gap, misread or doubted. Do not skip this — the pattern that emerges over three papers is worth more than the papers themselves.
  3. Tally by unit, not by paper. Count how many questions came from criticism, how many from drama, how many from language. Compare that with how you are spending your study hours. The mismatch is usually startling.
  4. Rework the paper a week later. Not to score better — to check whether the corrections held. Anything you get wrong twice needs a different method, not more repetition.
  5. Keep a single error notebook across all papers. In the last week before your examination, this notebook is the most valuable document you own.

Where to get authentic papers

Use official sources only. Question papers circulating on messaging apps are frequently mistyped, mixed between years, or carry wrong answer keys — and a confidently wrong answer key is worse than no practice at all.

  • UGC-NET — question papers and answer keys are released by the National Testing Agency on its official portal after each cycle.
  • University papers — most universities publish past papers in their examination or student section. For CCSU, papers are archived by course code.
  • IGNOU — term-end examination papers are available through the university's own question paper repository.
