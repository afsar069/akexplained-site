What the TGT English exam is

TGT stands for Trained Graduate Teacher. A TGT teaches English up to the secondary level — broadly Classes VI to X. The qualification required is a graduate degree in English together with a B.Ed, and the paper is pitched at graduate rather than postgraduate level.

That difference matters. Compared with PGT, the TGT paper leans harder on language accuracy and rewards precision in grammar as much as breadth in literature.

Who conducts it

  • KVS — Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan
  • NVS — Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti
  • DSSSB — Delhi Subordinate Services Selection Board
  • UPSESSB — Uttar Pradesh Secondary Education Services Selection Board
  • State boards and Public Service Commissions

Each sets its own paper. The pattern below holds across most of them, but the notification for your board is the final word.

How the paper is usually built

Most TGT English papers fall into two clear halves, sometimes with a general section attached.

Section I — Language

AreaWhat is tested
ComprehensionAn unseen passage with questions on meaning, inference and vocabulary in context
Parts of speechNouns, pronouns, verbs, adjectives, adverbs, prepositions, conjunctions
Tense and agreementCorrect tense use, subject–verb agreement, sequence of tenses
Voice and narrationActive and passive, direct and indirect speech
TransformationSimple, compound and complex sentences; synthesis
VocabularySynonyms, antonyms, one-word substitution, idioms and phrases
MechanicsSpelling, punctuation, common errors

This section is entirely learnable. Marks lost here are lost carelessly, and they are the easiest to recover.

Section II — Literature

The literature half is narrower than the PGT paper but still substantial:

  • Forms of literature — poetry, prose, drama and fiction, and what distinguishes each
  • Literary devices and figures of speech — simile, metaphor, personification, alliteration, irony, hyperbole, oxymoron, apostrophe
  • History of English literature — the ages in order, with their defining features and representative writers
  • Major authors and their works — Shakespeare, Milton, Wordsworth, Coleridge, Shelley, Keats, Charles Lamb, Dickens, Hardy, Tennyson, Browning, Shaw, Eliot, and Indian writers in English including Tagore and Kamala Das
  • Critical appreciation — reading a short passage or poem and commenting on its theme, tone and technique

General section

Several boards add a separate paper covering general awareness, reasoning, teaching aptitude and pedagogy. It is usually smaller than the subject paper but rarely negligible in a close contest.

Where candidates lose marks

Treating grammar as revision rather than study. Graduates who read literature comfortably often assume the language section will take care of itself. It will not. Narration, transformation and one-word substitution reward drilling, and nothing else.

Learning writers without periods. A name attached to no age is a name that will not survive the exam hall. Fix the timeline first; the writers then have somewhere to sit.

Skipping figures of speech. They are simple, they are certain to appear, and they are frequently the fastest marks on the paper.

Download the official notification and syllabus for your board before you plan anything. Duration, number of questions, negative marking and prescribed material differ, and they change between cycles.

What you will find here

Notes on every literary age, explanations of the terms and devices that repeat year after year, and practice sets — in clear English and in Hinglish, whichever you think in. If you are starting cold, begin with the history of English literature and work forward in order.
