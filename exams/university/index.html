University Examinations

Descriptive papers need a different kind of preparation from NET. Choose your university and work from its own syllabus.

Why a separate section for university papers

A UGC-NET aspirant and a BA second-semester student are reading the same literature, but they are not preparing for the same paper. University examinations are descriptive, they follow one particular institution's prescribed texts, and they reward a completely different kind of answer — a structured essay rather than a two-mark recognition.

So the material here is organised by university, not by topic. Choose yours, and you will see only the papers that appear on your own question paper.

What applies to every university paper

Whichever institution you belong to, descriptive answers are judged on the same four things, and most students lose marks on the last two rather than the first.

  1. Answer the question that was asked. A brilliant essay on the themes of a play scores badly if the question asked about its structure. Underline the operative word in the question — discuss, examine, compare, justify — before you write a single line.
  2. Argue, do not narrate. Retelling the plot is the most common way of filling three pages and earning six marks. State a position in the opening paragraph and spend the rest of the answer defending it.
  3. Quote briefly and precisely. Two short accurate quotations are worth more than a page of half-remembered lines. If you are unsure of the wording, paraphrase and name the scene instead.
  4. Finish. An unfinished answer loses more marks than a shorter complete one. Divide the total time by the number of questions before you start, and stop when your time for a question is up, even mid-thought.
